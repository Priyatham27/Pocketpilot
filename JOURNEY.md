# 🚀 The Story of PocketPilot

*"Every great product begins with a simple problem."*

---

## Chapter 1 — The Problem

Like many students and young professionals, I often found myself asking simple questions that surprisingly had no simple answer.

How much money do I actually have?

Can I afford this purchase?

How much do I still owe?

Who owes me money?

Can I go on that trip next month?

Am I saving enough?

Most finance applications could tell me where my money went.

Very few could help me decide what to do next.

That realization sparked the idea for PocketPilot.

---

## Chapter 2 — From Budget Tracker to Finance OS

The original vision was incredibly small.

A simple application where I could:

* Record income
* Record expenses
* Track debts

Nothing more.

But while brainstorming, I realized something.

People don't actually need another expense tracker.

People need clarity.

Instead of building another ledger, I started designing a system that organizes every aspect of personal finance.

That single shift changed the entire project.

PocketPilot stopped being a Budget Tracker.

It became a Personal Finance Operating System.

---

## Chapter 7 — The Technical Evolution

The first version stored everything locally.

Fast.

Offline.

Simple.

Once the experience felt polished, the architecture evolved.

Local Storage became Cloud Storage.

Authentication was introduced.

Every piece of data became securely associated with its owner.

The application transformed into a real cloud platform.

---

## Chapter 8 — The Biggest Challenge

During deployment, Google authentication appeared to work.

Users authenticated successfully.

But every login redirected back to the login page.

Instead of applying random fixes, the authentication architecture was rebuilt correctly using the official Supabase App Router flow.

Authentication.

Sessions.

Security.

Protected routes.

Everything was rebuilt properly.

That moment transformed PocketPilot from a prototype into a production-ready application.
